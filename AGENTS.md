# Prototype Instructions

Run the local server yourself and open the preview in the browser available to this environment. Do not give the user server-start instructions when you can run it.

Before making substantial visual changes, use the Product Design plugin's `get-context` skill when the visual source is unclear or no longer matches the current goal. When the user gives durable prototype-specific design feedback, preferences, or decisions, record them in `AGENTS.md`.

When implementing from a selected generated mock, treat that image as the source of truth for layout, component anatomy, density, spacing, color, typography, visible content, and hierarchy.

## Durable prototype decisions

- The selected visual source is `design-reference/option-2-guided-practice.png` (the second ideation result).
- Preserve its warm Singapore heartland setting, over-the-shoulder consultation view, Explore → Assess → Plan rail, teal/mint/terracotta palette, and restrained coaching HUD.
- The product must remain a real-time 3D training game with playable clinical choices; do not flatten the experience into a static reproduction of the reference image.
- Keep the consultation camera slightly elevated with a gentle downward viewing angle; avoid a low, counter-height perspective.
- Exploration must be playable through player movement, proximity-gated patient conversations, and visible idle, walk, speech, and reaction animation while retaining the real-time 3D consultation.
- The foreground must read as a finished real pharmacy floor rather than an exposed white stage, and players must have constrained camera-angle presets that preserve the elevated guided composition.
- The pharmacy room and visible exterior must be built from 3D geometry; do not use a large photographic backdrop to simulate architectural depth.
- Give patients gender-appropriate voices and keep the player on one specific, consistent voice; the player and patient must not intentionally share a character voice.
